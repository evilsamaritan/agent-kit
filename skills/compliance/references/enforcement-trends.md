# Enforcement Trends

Volatile enforcement data — dates, fines, regulatory trends. Update periodically.

## GDPR Enforcement (2025-2026)

- **Cumulative fines:** ~7.1B EUR since May 2018
- **Annual pace:** ~1.2B EUR/year (stabilized since 2024)
- **60%+ of all fines** imposed since January 2023
- **Breach notifications:** 400+/day average (22% increase YoY)
- **2,800+ fines** issued since GDPR inception

### Notable Fines (2025-2026)

| Entity | Amount | Reason |
|--------|--------|--------|
| TikTok | 530M EUR | Improper transfer of user data to China |
| Meta | 479M EUR | Unlawful processing of user data |
| Google LLC | 200M EUR | Ads disguised as emails in Gmail without consent |
| SHEIN | 150M EUR | Cookies placed without valid consent |

### Focus Areas

- **Dark patterns:** regulators actively test websites, not just respond to complaints
- **Cookie consent:** making rejection harder than acceptance = violation
- **AI processing:** EDPB clarifies legitimate interest can apply but with full safeguards
- **Healthcare:** average penalties jumped significantly due to missing DPIAs
- **Cross-sector expansion:** enforcement now targets SMEs, retail, energy — not just Big Tech
- **Personal liability:** regulators hint at holding executives personally liable

### Proposed GDPR Amendments (Q4 2025)

- One-click cookie reject with equal prominence to accept
- SME exemption for Records of Processing expanded from 250 to 750 employees
- Clarified AI processing provisions

## EU AI Act — Key Dates

| Date | What becomes enforceable |
|------|------------------------|
| Aug 1, 2024 | AI Act entered into force |
| Feb 2, 2025 | Prohibited AI practices (social scoring, real-time biometric ID, emotion recognition at work/school, predictive policing based solely on profiling) |
| Aug 2, 2025 | GPAI obligations, AI literacy requirements, governance rules |
| Aug 2, 2026 | High-risk AI systems (Annex III): employment, credit, education, law enforcement |
| Aug 2, 2027 | High-risk AI embedded in regulated products (extended transition) |

### Penalty Structure

| Violation type | Maximum penalty |
|---------------|----------------|
| Prohibited AI practices | 35M EUR or 7% global turnover |
| High-risk non-compliance | 15M EUR or 3% global turnover |
| Incorrect information to authorities | 7.5M EUR or 1% global turnover |

### Digital Omnibus Proposal (Late 2025)

European Commission proposed potentially postponing Annex III high-risk obligations to December 2027. Status uncertain — treat August 2026 as the binding deadline.

## Cross-Border Transfer Developments (2025-2026)

- **US-EU Data Privacy Framework (DPF):** active, enables EU-US transfers for certified companies
- **UK adequacy:** EDPB Opinion 2025/26 extended UK adequacy until 2031
- **US DOJ bulk data rule (April 2025):** prohibits sharing American sensitive data with countries of concern (China, Russia, Iran, North Korea, Cuba, Venezuela)
- **UK Data (Use and Access) Act 2025:** introduces risk-based comparative assessment for transfers
- **EU Data Act (Sept 2025):** extends sovereignty to non-personal and industrial data
- **Vietnam Law on Data (July 2025):** government approval required for core data exports
- **China PIPL:** continues to require local storage with restricted cross-border transfers

## Children's Privacy (2025-2026)

- **COPPA 2.0 amendments (effective June 2025):** first major overhaul since 2013
  - Separate consent for advertising vs. core service functions
  - Expanded PII definition: biometric data, geolocation, behavioral data
  - Heightened data security and retention obligations
  - Most compliance obligations effective April 2026
- **FTC age verification policy (Feb 2026):** safe harbor for mixed-audience sites using age verification
- **EU:** enforcement intensifying around children's data, especially in gaming and social media

---

## Privacy Standards

- **ISO 27701:2025 (Oct 2025):** major revision — now standalone (no ISO 27001 prerequisite), 3-year transition to Oct 2028
- **NIST Privacy Framework:** under review (April 2025), aligning with Cybersecurity Framework 2.0
- **Evidence-based accountability:** auditors and regulators now expect operational evidence (automated data maps, DPIAs, AI risk assessments), not just policies

---

## Trend Summary

1. Enforcement is broadening (beyond Big Tech) and accelerating
2. AI governance is the fastest-growing compliance domain
3. Data sovereignty is replacing borderless data flows as the default
4. Children's privacy regulations are expanding globally
5. Compliance is shifting from policy-based to evidence-based accountability
6. Cross-border transfer mechanisms are fragmenting by jurisdiction
